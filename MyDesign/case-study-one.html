Stray Cat Alliance

PROJECT OVERVIEW We chose to redesign the Non-Profit Organization Stray Cat Alliance. As a group. Our love for cats was very apparent within our first group meeting. As we browsed some non- profit cat organization websites, we saw the most opportunity to really make an impactful design change with Stray Cat Alliance.

Research

Value Proposition and Customer Segment

We concluded that users would interact with SCA to volunteer, adopt, or meet like minded people who wanted to help the growing population of stray cats in L.A.. The value that SCA offers to these users is opportunities to volunteer at their organization while simultaneously helping Stray Cats with other animal lovers. SCA also offers potential adopters with options to "Adopt Don't Shop"

View

Persona

Navigation Bar Competitor Analysis

SCA's site navigation left much to be desired. We wanted to redesign the hierarchy of their main navigation menu to better serve their purpose. We analyzed the main navigation of the biggest animal non-profit sites (Humane Society, ASPCA, etc.) to see what was working for them and what was not, so that we could re-design SCA's navigation to improve user experience and their impact in the community.

View

Card Sorting - Before

The current SCA navigation gave importance to things like the non-profits history and founders, as opposed to what they were trying to do as an organization. Things like adoption and fostering were much more difficult to find than info about the founder. The non-profit also has extensive information on how to take care of Cat Colonies, but it is buried so far in the site that users wouldn't know unless they accidentally stumbled upon it (As we did)

View Image

Card Sorting - After

We streamlined the navigation of the site by including less drop down menus, eliminating jargon not understood by most users, and strategically placing links in menus where they made sense.
